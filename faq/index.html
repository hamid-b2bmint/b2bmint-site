01 — Methodology

How SAGE actually works

What is SAGE?+

SAGE stands for Search & AI Growth Engine. It’s our 3-stage framework: build semantic authority, earn search and AI visibility, then convert that into pipeline. Built on Koray Tuğberk GUBUR’s semantic SEO methodology, adapted for B2B SaaS and AI search.

How is this different from what other SaaS SEO agencies do?+

Most SaaS SEO agencies work from keyword lists, publish blog posts, and report traffic. SAGE starts from a semantic map of your category — the entities, relationships, and questions buyers actually search. Then we build content as a connected system, optimize for both Google and AI models simultaneously, and report on pipeline, not traffic.

The practical difference: one engine handles SEO and GEO because 80% of the work overlaps. Agencies that sell them separately are double-billing you.

What is the 71-rule framework?+

A structural quality check every page runs through before publishing. It covers entity density, co-occurrence signals, answer formatting, schema markup, internal link geometry, and the specific attributes Google and LLMs reward when deciding who to rank and who to cite. Not style guidelines. Structural rules.

Do you do link building?+

Yes, when strategy calls for it. The semantic foundation does most of the heavy lifting. Strategic backlinks from relevant sources accelerate authority when competition requires it. We don’t do generic guest-post spam.

03 — AI Citations & GEO

Getting cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overviews

How do we get cited by ChatGPT?+

Four things LLMs reward: entity recognition (they need to see your brand as a distinct entity), information uniqueness (content that says something the web doesn’t already say 50 times), structured extractability (content that can be pulled into answers), and topical authority (semantic coverage of your category). SAGE engineers all four. One client went from zero to 4,244 citations in 6 months.

What’s the difference between GEO and AEO?+

GEO (generative engine optimization) and AEO (answer engine optimization) describe the same discipline with slightly different framing. In practice, the strategies are identical. We use GEO because the “answer” frame under-represents how LLMs generate responses.

How do you measure AI visibility?+

Platform-by-platform citation monitoring across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, AI Overviews, and Copilot. We track how often your brand is cited, for which queries, and alongside which competitors. AI referral traffic is captured in GA4 separately.

Do I need GEO if I already have good SEO?+

Good SEO is a head start, not a complete strategy. If your SEO is genuinely strong, you’re 80% of the way there. SAGE adds the 20% specific to AI visibility: entity recognition engineering, prompt research, content structured for LLM extraction, and citation monitoring. The mistake is treating GEO as a separate budget — the work overlaps.
