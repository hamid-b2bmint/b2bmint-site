About B2BMint

Built to Fix What's Broken in SaaS SEO

B2BMint exists because most SaaS companies spend real money on SEO and get traffic reports instead of pipeline. That's not an execution problem. It's a methodology problem.

The Origin

The same pattern. Every time.

I spent years inside the SaaS growth ecosystem watching the same pattern repeat. Company raises funding. Hires SEO agency. Agency publishes 50-80 blog posts. Traffic goes up. Pipeline stays flat. Agency blames the product, the market, or the timeline. Company moves on and repeats with the next agency.

The problem was never effort. The agencies worked hard. The writers wrote. The reports were detailed. But the underlying methodology was fundamentally wrong.

They built content without architecture. Targeted keywords without understanding entities. Optimized for Google without acknowledging that half their buyers had shifted to AI search. And measured success with traffic numbers that nobody in the boardroom cared about.

I kept seeing companies with genuinely better products lose to competitors with weaker offerings but stronger search authority. Not because those competitors spent more. Because their content was structured as an interconnected system that Google and AI models recognized as authoritative.

B2BMint was built to close that gap.

Hamid Khan Founder, B2BMint
The Methodology Behind SAGE

Built on the most technically rigorous approach to search authority that exists today.

SAGE is built on Koray Tugberk GUBUR's semantic SEO methodology, adapted and expanded for B2B SaaS and AI search.

Most SEO agencies work from a keyword list. SAGE works from a semantic map of entities, contextual vectors, and topical relationships that mirror how search engines and large language models actually understand authority.

The methodology spans 9 interconnected modules, from initial semantic architecture through entity consolidation and AI citation positioning. Each module builds on the last. Skip one and the compounding effect breaks.

We apply a 71-rule quality framework to every piece of content. Not style guidelines. Structural rules that determine whether Google and AI models will treat a page as authoritative or disposable.

We analyzed 23 SaaS SEO agencies. Not one explains their underlying methodology publicly. Because most don't have one beyond "write content, build links, wait."
